# Getting Started

This guide walks you through placing your first prediction on Vision. By the end, you will have joined a batch, placed UP/DOWN bets on real markets, and understand how to claim rewards.

## Prerequisites

You need:
- An **Arbitrum wallet** (MetaMask, Rabby, or any EVM-compatible wallet)
- **USDC on Arbitrum** (6 decimals -- this is standard Arbitrum USDC)

<Warning>
Vision runs on **Arbitrum** (chain ID 421611337), not the Index L3 chain. Make sure your wallet is connected to Arbitrum and you have USDC there.
</Warning>

## Step 1: Browse Active Batches

Navigate to [generalmarket.io/vision](https://generalmarket.io/vision). The Vision page lists all active batches. Each batch shows:

- **Markets** -- the assets being tracked (e.g., BTC/USD, ETH/USD, SOL/USD)
- **Tick duration** -- how often the batch resolves (5 min, 1 hour, 1 day, etc.)
- **Player count** -- how many players are currently in the batch
- **TVL** -- total USDC deposited across all players

Click on any batch to expand its details and see per-market stats.

## Step 2: Understand a Batch

Each batch is a self-contained prediction game with these parameters:

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Markets** | The asset pairs players predict on (e.g., BTC/USD, ETH/USD) |
| **Tick duration** | The time interval between resolutions (5 min to 30 days) |
| **Resolution type** | How outcomes are determined -- e.g., UP_0 (any upward move), UP_30 (must move +30bps) |
| **Min stake** | Minimum USDC per tick (0.1 USDC) |

<Tip>
Start with a batch that has a longer tick duration (1 hour or 1 day) so you have time to observe how resolution works before trying faster intervals.
</Tip>

## Step 3: Place Your Predictions

For each market in the batch, choose **UP** or **DOWN**:

- **UP** -- you predict the asset price will be higher at the end of the tick
- **DOWN** -- you predict the asset price will be lower at the end of the tick

Your predictions are combined into a **bitmap** -- a compact binary encoding where 1 = UP and 0 = DOWN. The bitmap is hashed and submitted on-chain so nobody can see your bets before the tick closes.

## Step 4: Deposit USDC and Join

1. Set your **deposit amount** -- the total USDC to allocate to this batch.
2. Set your **stake per tick** -- how much USDC to wager per tick interval. Your deposit covers multiple ticks.
3. **Approve** the Vision contract to spend your USDC (first time only).
4. **Confirm** the join transaction. Your bitmap hash and deposit are recorded on-chain.

<Info>
Your deposit is split across ticks automatically. If you deposit 10 USDC with a 1 USDC stake per tick, you are covered for 10 ticks. You can top up or withdraw at any time.
</Info>

## Step 5: Wait for Tick Resolution

At the end of each tick interval, issuers fetch live prices and determine the outcome for each market:

- If the price went **up** and you bet **UP**, you win that market.
- If the price went **down** and you bet **DOWN**, you win that market.
- Winners on each market **split the losers' stakes** proportionally, minus a 0.3% fee on profits.

Your balance updates automatically after each tick. Check the batch detail page to see per-tick results and your running PnL.

## Step 6: Claim Rewards

When you are ready to cash out:

1. Navigate to **My Positions** in the Vision page.
2. Click **Withdraw** on the batch you want to exit.
3. The issuer network generates a **BLS-signed balance proof** for your current balance.
4. Confirm the withdrawal transaction. Your USDC is returned to your wallet.

<Info>
You can also claim partial amounts -- you do not need to exit the batch entirely. Withdrawn funds are available in your wallet immediately.
</Info>

## Programmatic Access

Vision has a full REST API for bots and scripts. Here are two common operations:

### Fetch Active Batches

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="cURL">
    ```bash
    curl -s https://generalmarket.io/api/vision/batches | jq .
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Python">
    ```python
    import requests

    resp = requests.get("https://generalmarket.io/api/vision/batches")
    batches = resp.json()

    for batch in batches:
        print(f"Batch {batch['id']}: {batch['market_count']} markets, "
              f"{batch['player_count']} players, TVL: {batch['tvl']}")
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="TypeScript">
    ```typescript
    const res = await fetch("https://generalmarket.io/api/vision/batches");
    const batches = await res.json();

    for (const batch of batches) {
      console.log(
        `Batch ${batch.id}: ${batch.market_count} markets, ` +
        `${batch.player_count} players, TVL: ${batch.tvl}`
      );
    }
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Check Your Balance

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="cURL">
    ```bash
    BATCH_ID=1
    PLAYER=0xYourAddress
    curl -s "https://generalmarket.io/api/vision/balance/$BATCH_ID/$PLAYER" | jq .
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Python">
    ```python
    import requests

    batch_id = 1
    player = "0xYourAddress"
    resp = requests.get(
        f"https://generalmarket.io/api/vision/balance/{batch_id}/{player}"
    )
    balance = resp.json()
    print(f"Balance: {balance['balance']} (USDC raw, 6 decimals)")
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="TypeScript">
    ```typescript
    const batchId = 1;
    const player = "0xYourAddress";
    const res = await fetch(
      `https://generalmarket.io/api/vision/balance/${batchId}/${player}`
    );
    const balance = await res.json();
    console.log(`Balance: ${balance.balance} (USDC raw, 6 decimals)`);
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
