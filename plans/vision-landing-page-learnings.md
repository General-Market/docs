## Arguments NOT Optimized — Do Not Reuse As-Is
The conversion arguments in these pages were drafted but NOT validated or optimized. Key issues:

1. **"19 traders per market" framing is BAD** — makes the platform sound dead/empty. Never lead with low participant counts.
2. **Stat-based pitches all felt the same** — v2 pages (10 of them) all used the same stat-first approach with different headlines. Felt repetitive, not genuinely different.
3. **4-section conversion formula killed narrative** — research said "fewer sections convert better" but the result was skeleton pages with no soul. Rich, deep pages with real narrative perform better subjectively.
4. **Headlines need work** — most headlines were generic or tried too hard to be clever. The manifesto's bold statements worked better than stat-laden headlines.
5. **"Trade 100k markets with $0.10"** — user preferred this framing over "Start with a dollar. Stay because it works." Emphasizes breadth + low barrier simultaneously.

## What Worked
- **Narrative-driven pages** (story, deep dive) resonated more than data-driven ones
- **The "What You Know → What You Trade" mapping visual** was explicitly liked by user
- **Bold statements with proof** (manifesto pattern) felt confident, not salesy
- **Cinematic one-market-story → zoom-out** was compelling (deep dive)
- **Rich, deep pages** (1,500-3,000 lines) are better than skeleton pages

## What To Do Next
- Arguments in the 3 surviving pages need optimization pass
- Headlines need A/B testing
- The conversion arguments from `vision-conversion-arguments.md` were research-based but not all landed — need to validate which specific arguments actually move people
- Consider combining: manifesto's rhetoric + deep dive's storytelling + story's identity mapping
