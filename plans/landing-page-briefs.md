# Vision Landing Page — 3 Propositions

## Goal
Convert 20% of visitors into making their first trade on Vision (sealed parimutuel prediction markets with 1,000+ markets across 44 data sources).

---

## Page 1: "Trade What You Know" (Knowledge Arbitrage)
**File:** `landing-page-1-knowledge-edge.html`

**Core narrative:** You already spend hours on 4chan, Hacker News, tracking npm downloads, watching anime. That niche knowledge is worthless on Binance — everyone trades the same BTC/ETH. On Vision, your internet obsessions become your trading edge.

**Headline:** "Your Internet Addiction Finally Pays Off"

**Target audience:** Internet power users, developers, degens, anime fans, crypto natives who consume niche content daily.

**Conversion hook:** Identity-based. "You're already an expert. This is where that expertise becomes profit." Makes the visitor feel smart and special.

**Key sections:**
- 4 persona cards (the /biz/ Degen, the Dev, the Anime Fan, the News Junkie)
- Comparison table: Binance vs Polymarket vs Vision
- Interactive terminal showing actual CLI commands
- Market explorer grid with 12 categories + sparklines

**Why it might work:** Flatters the user. Creates FOMO ("I already know this stuff, why aren't I trading it?"). Low psychological barrier because it reframes existing behavior as profitable.

**Risk:** Too niche? Might not resonate with mainstream traders who don't identify as "internet addicts."

---

## Page 2: "Everything Is a Market" (Spectacle & Breadth)
**File:** `landing-page-2-everything-is-a-market.html`

**Core narrative:** What if you could bet on ANYTHING? Volcanic eruptions. ISS altitude. 4chan shitpost volume. NYC subway ridership. Military aircraft. This is the most fascinating prediction market ever built — a Bloomberg terminal for reality itself.

**Headline:** "BET ON EVERYTHING"

**Target audience:** Curious, adventurous traders. People bored of traditional markets. The "I'd bet on that" crowd. Viral-shareable audience.

**Conversion hook:** Awe and curiosity. The sheer breadth is the wow factor. You open the page and think "wait, I can bet on VOLCANOES?"

**Key sections:**
- 8 color-coded category bands (Crypto, Tech, Entertainment, 4chan, Earth, Space, Macro, Transport)
- Each with 3 live-looking market cards
- Animated card-flip showing sealed bet hash
- Scrolling ticker with 17 live market feeds
- Final CTA with 6 exotic market cards

**Why it might work:** Viral potential. People share this because it's mind-blowing. The "holy shit" factor drives first clicks. Bloomberg-terminal aesthetic signals sophistication.

**Risk:** Might feel gimmicky. "Bet on volcanoes" could undermine trust. Spectacle without substance.

---

## Page 3: "Your Edge, Protected" (Serious Trader)
**File:** `landing-page-3-serious-trader.html`

**Core narrative:** You've been burned by front-runners on Binance, whale manipulation on Polymarket, PFOF on Robinhood. Vision uses sealed commitments — your bets are hashed before anyone sees them. No copying. No front-running. If you can code, you can automate. Skill wins here.

**Headline:** "Stop Getting Front-Run."

**Target audience:** Quantitative traders, programmers, competitive degens who've been burned. People who think "if I could just find a fair market."

**Conversion hook:** Pain point. Every trader has been front-run or manipulated. This speaks directly to that frustration and offers a technical solution.

**Key sections:**
- The Problem: 3 columns exposing CEX, Polymarket, and Robinhood flaws
- 3 Pillars: Sealed Commitments, Parimutuel, BLS Verification
- "Built for Bots" with Python code examples (MomentumStrategy backtest)
- Performance metrics (5min resolution, 0.3% fee, 0.1 USDC min)
- Trader testimonials (The Quant, The Degen, The Developer)

**Why it might work:** Builds trust through technical transparency. Code snippets signal "this is real." Addresses real pain points that serious traders feel daily. The "bots welcome" angle is unique — most platforms ban bots.

**Risk:** Too technical for casual users. Might filter out the "I'll throw 5 bucks at it" crowd who are actually easier to convert.

## Recommendation

| Criterion | Page 1 (Knowledge) | Page 2 (Everything) | Page 3 (Serious) |
|-----------|:-------------------:|:--------------------:|:-----------------:|
| Viral shareability | Medium | **High** | Low |
| Trust building | Medium | Low | **High** |
| First-trade conversion | **High** | Medium | Medium |
| Broad appeal | Medium | **High** | Low |
| Trader retention | Medium | Low | **High** |
| Technical credibility | Medium | Medium | **High** |

**For 20% first-trade conversion:** Page 1 is likely strongest — it meets users where they are and makes trading feel like a natural extension of what they already do.

**For viral growth:** Page 2 — the spectacle drives shares and press coverage.

**For high-value traders:** Page 3 — attracts users who trade more and stay longer.

**Hybrid approach:** Use Page 2's hero (the spectacle hook) with Page 1's persona section (identity-based conversion) and Page 3's technical credibility (sealed bets + code). This combines: awe → identity → trust → action.
