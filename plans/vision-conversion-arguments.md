# PART 0: THE 20% CONVERSION FRAMEWORK

Why current pages convert at 5%: too much marketing copy, no product, no interactive demo, too many sections, wrong reading level, wallet-only onboarding.

## The Formula (research-backed)

Pages that hit 20% share exactly this structure:

```
SECTION 1: HERO
- One-line value prop (7th-grade reading level)
- Live data counter (volume/users/markets)
- Primary CTA + secondary CTA
- THE PRODUCT visible or embedded (not a screenshot — the real thing)

SECTION 2: VALUE PROOF (pick 1-2 arguments, not 7)
- 2-3 specific numbers with proof
- NOT feature lists, NOT before/afters, NOT comparisons
- Social proof AT this decision point (next to CTA)

SECTION 3: INTERACTIVE DEMO (the single biggest lever)
- Ungated (no email, no wallet to browse)
- 5-12 steps showing a simulated first trade
- Users who interact: +70% signup, +80% activation
- 7.2x more engagement than video

SECTION 4: FINAL CTA
- Same action as hero
- "Endowed progress" framing: "You're 1 step away"
- Risk reversal pills underneath (0.1 USDC, no KYC, etc.)
```

That's it. 4 sections. Not 10. Not 13.

## Key Numbers

| Finding | Data |
|---------|------|
| Pages with <10 elements convert 2x better than 40+ | KlientBoost |
| 7th-grade copy: 11.1% conversion. College: 5.3% | Unbounce |
| Interactive demos: +70% signup, 7.2x vs video | Navattic 2025 |
| Ungated demos: +10% engagement vs gated | Navattic 2025 |
| Embedded wallets + social login: +20-60% conversion | Alchemy |
| Each extra form field cuts conversion ~10% | Shopify |
| Each extra second of load time costs 7% | DebugBear |
| 3 CTA placements (hero/mid/end) beats 1 | CXL |

## What We Were Doing Wrong

1. **10+ sections of arguments before CTA** — should be 4 max
2. **Marketing copy instead of product** — Uniswap homepage IS the swap. Hyperliquid has no marketing page.
3. **No interactive demo** — this alone could 3x conversion
4. **College-level copy** — halves conversion vs 7th-grade
5. **Arguments as content** — before/afters are good FOR ARGUMENTS but the page should be product-first, argument-supported
6. **Wallet connect only** — need email/social login option (+20-60%)

## How to Use the Arguments Below

The before/afters, stacks, and timeline are STILL the validated conversion arguments. But they deploy differently on a 20% page:

- **Pick MAX 2 arguments per page** (not all 10)
- **Arguments support the product demo, not replace it**
- **Arguments appear BETWEEN hero CTA and demo**, as quick proof
- **Each argument is 1 visual + 1 sentence**, not a full section
- **The strongest argument appears as the hero subheadline**, not its own section

## The Product-First Principle

The interactive market grid (live data, sparklines, trader counts, category tabs) IS the pitch. On Uniswap, the swap widget sells the product. On Vision, the market grid sells the product. The grid shows:
- Weird market names (volcanoes, ISS, 4chan) → curiosity
- Low trader counts (4, 7, 11) → opportunity
- Live updating data → credibility
- Category breadth → depth

A visitor who spends 30 seconds browsing the grid is 70% more likely to connect a wallet than one who reads 13 sections of arguments.

# PART 4: ARGUMENT DEPLOYMENT RULES (20% framework)

## Per-Page Budget

Each landing page gets a MAX budget of:
- **1 hero argument** (appears as headline/subheadline, not a section)
- **1-2 support arguments** (compact, between hero and demo)
- **1 interactive demo** (the actual product grid)
- **0 full-section arguments** (no dedicated "before/after" sections)

## How Arguments Compress

Each argument must fit in ONE of these formats:

**Format A: Hero stat** (used for hero headline)
```
19 traders per market. Polymarket averages 1,200.
```
One line. Two numbers. That's the argument.

**Format B: Quick proof** (used between hero and demo)
```
┌──────────────────────────────────┐
│ YOUR KNOWLEDGE     YOUR MARKETS  │
│ npm trends    ───→ npm downloads │
│ 4chan culture  ───→ /biz/ velocity│
│ anime seasons ───→ AniList ratings│
│ 8 of 8 match on Vision.         │
│ 0 of 8 on Polymarket.           │
└──────────────────────────────────┘
```
One compact card. Not a full-width section.

**Format C: Inline proof** (used as caption under market grid)
```
"See a market you understand? The average one has 19 traders. You already have edge."
```
One sentence next to the product.

## Best Pairings

| Page Archetype | Hero Argument | Support Argument |
|----------------|---------------|------------------|
| AI-focused | "19 traders/market" (Crowd) | Learning Speed (4 vs 8,640) |
| Identity-focused | "Your day = 8 markets" (Your Day) | Know vs Trade (8/8 match) |
| Discovery-focused | "1,000+ markets" (The Screen) | Inline: trader counts in grid |
| FOMO-focused | "19 going up" (Countdown) | Staleness (zero research) |
| Comparison-focused | "Polymarket vs Vision" (Split) | Surface Area (trader counts) |

## What NEVER Gets Its Own Section

- How It Works (compress to 1 line under demo)
- Strategy privacy / sealed bets (1 sentence, not a section)
- Risk reversal pills (under CTA, not standalone)
- Social proof / leaderboard (inside the product grid, not separate)
- Code examples (inside demo, not separate)
- 6-month timeline (too long for a 4-section page — compress to 2 sentences)
