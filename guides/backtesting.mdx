General Market's backtesting engine simulates how a portfolio strategy would have performed historically. Users can test a thesis, compare configurations, and deploy strategies directly as live Index Tracking Products (ITPs).

---

## Getting Started

Navigate to the **Backtest** section from the main menu. The backtesting flow has three stages: configure the strategy, run the simulation, and review results.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a Category">
    Choose an asset category to define the universe of tokens the strategy can draw from. Categories include:
    - **Made in China** -- tokens from Chinese-origin projects
    - **AI Tokens** -- artificial intelligence and machine learning protocols
    - **DeFi Blue Chips** -- established decentralized finance protocols
    - **Layer 1s** -- base-layer blockchain tokens
    - **Memecoins** -- community-driven tokens
    - And many more

    The category determines the pool of assets available for the strategy to select from.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure Parameters">
    Set the core parameters for the strategy:

    | Parameter | Description |
    |-----------|-------------|
    | **Top N Assets** | How many assets to include (e.g., top 10 by market cap from the selected category) |
    | **Weighting Strategy** | How to allocate across selected assets (see below) |
    | **Rebalance Frequency** | How often to recalculate weights (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly) |
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run Simulation">
    Click **Run Backtest** to start the simulation. The backtesting engine replays historical price data and applies the strategy rules at each rebalance period. Results typically appear within a few seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Weighting Strategies

Choose how capital is allocated across the selected assets:

### Equal Weight

Every asset receives the same allocation. Simple and effective for broad diversification.

```text
weight[i] = 1 / N
```

### Market Cap Weighted

Assets are weighted proportionally to their market capitalization. Larger projects get larger allocations, similar to traditional index funds like the S&P 500.

```text
weight[i] = marketCap[i] / sum(marketCap)
```

### Multi-Factor

A composite score combining multiple signals to determine weights. Factors can include market cap, volume, momentum, and volatility. Higher-scoring assets receive larger allocations.

## Advanced Features

### Sweep Mode

Compare multiple configurations side by side. Sweep mode lets you vary one or more parameters across a range and see how each combination performs.

For example, sweep the **Top N** parameter from 5 to 50 in steps of 5 to find the optimal number of holdings for the selected category.

<Tip>
Sweep mode is the fastest way to find the optimal configuration. Instead of running backtests one at a time, sweep mode tests dozens of combinations in a single run.
</Tip>

### Market Signal Integrations

Layer additional data signals into the strategy:

| Signal | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Fear & Greed Index** | Adjust allocation based on market sentiment. Reduce exposure during extreme greed, increase during extreme fear. |
| **BTC Dominance** | Shift between altcoins and BTC-heavy allocations based on Bitcoin's share of total market cap. |
| **VC Funding Data** | Filter for tokens backed by major venture capital firms, or exclude heavily VC-funded tokens. |

These signals can be used as filters (include/exclude assets) or as weighting modifiers (increase/decrease allocation).

## Understanding Results

After a backtest completes, the backtesting engine generates a comprehensive performance report:

### Key Metrics

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Total Return** | Cumulative percentage gain or loss over the backtest period |
| **Max Drawdown** | Largest peak-to-trough decline -- measures the worst-case downside |
| **Sharpe Ratio** | Risk-adjusted return. Higher is better; above 1.0 is generally good, above 2.0 is excellent |
| **Annualized Return** | Return normalized to a yearly rate for easy comparison |

### Performance Charts

- **Equity curve** -- portfolio value over time
- **Drawdown chart** -- underwater plot showing periods of decline
- **Benchmark comparison** -- how the strategy compares against holding BTC or ETH

<Info>
Past performance does not guarantee future results. Backtesting uses historical data and cannot account for future market conditions, liquidity changes, or black swan events.
</Info>

## Deploy as ITP

A well-performing strategy can be deployed directly as a live ITP from the backtest results page.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review Final Weights">
    The backtest results show the current asset weights as of the most recent rebalance. Review the weights to confirm they match the intended allocation.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Deploy">
    Click the **Deploy as ITP** button. The button pre-fills the ITP creation form with the backtested assets and weights.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Metadata and Confirm">
    Add a name, description, and any optional metadata (website, video). Then confirm the deployment transaction. The strategy is now a live, tradable ITP on General Market.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The deployed ITP uses the weights from the backtest's final rebalance. Subsequent rebalances must be triggered manually or through the protocol's rebalance mechanism.
</Note>
