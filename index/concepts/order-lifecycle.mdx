# Order Lifecycle

Every buy or sell on General Market goes through a multi-step lifecycle: from order submission, through decentralized consensus, to real exchange execution and on-chain settlement. This page traces the full journey.

## Buy Flow: The 10 Steps

The following is the complete path of a buy order from the moment the buyer clicks "Buy" to the moment ITP tokens land in the buyer's wallet.

<Steps>
  <Step title="USDC Approval">
    The wallet approves the Index contract to spend USDC on the buyer's behalf. The USDC approval is a one-time action (or per-transaction, depending on the wallet's approval settings).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit Order">
    The buyer calls `submitOrder(itpId, amount, BUY)`. The buyer's USDC is transferred to the contract and **escrowed** -- the USDC is locked until the order fills or times out.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Order is PENDING">
    The order is recorded on-chain with status `PENDING`. The order is now visible to the issuer nodes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="COLLECT Phase">
    The issuer nodes detect the new pending order during the **COLLECT** phase of the current cycle (200ms). All pending orders are gathered into a candidate batch.
  </Step>
  <Step title="PROPOSE Phase">
    The cycle's **leader** (determined by round-robin election) proposes a batch containing all collected orders. The batch includes order IDs and the computed batch hash.
  </Step>
  <Step title="BLS Signing">
    Peer issuer nodes **validate** the proposed batch -- checking order data, prices, and consistency. Each validating issuer node signs the batch hash with the node's BLS private key.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Signature Aggregation">
    The leader collects BLS signatures from peer issuer nodes. Once the threshold is met (`ceil(2n/3)` of registered issuers), the leader aggregates the BLS signatures into a single compact proof.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Batch Confirmation">
    The leader calls `confirmBatch()` on-chain with the aggregated BLS signature. The contract verifies the signature against the registered aggregated public key. Orders move to `BATCHED` status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Exchange Execution">
    The Authorized Participant (AP) detects `TradeRequest` events from the batch confirmation. The AP executes the corresponding trades on **Bitget** exchange, buying the underlying assets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill Confirmation">
    The Authorized Participant (AP) calls `confirmFills()` on-chain with execution details. The contract validates fills against the **0.1% price tolerance**, then **mints ITP tokens** to the buyer's wallet. The order status becomes `FILLED`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Sell Flow

The sell flow mirrors the buy flow in reverse:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit Sell Order">
    The seller calls `submitOrder(itpId, amount, SELL)`. The seller's ITP tokens are escrowed by the contract.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Consensus and Batching">
    The sell order goes through the same COLLECT, PROPOSE, and BLS signing phases as a buy order.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Exchange Execution">
    The AP sells the underlying assets on Bitget.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Settlement">
    `confirmFills()` is called. The contract **burns** the seller's ITP tokens and **returns USDC** to the seller's wallet at the execution price.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Cycle Timing

The issuer nodes operate in continuous **1-second cycles**, each divided into 5 phases:

```text
|--- COLLECT ---|--- PROPOSE ---|--- SIGN_SUBMIT ---|--- CONFIRM ---|--- REBALANCE ---|
     200ms           200ms            200ms              200ms            200ms
```

| Phase | Duration | What Happens |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **COLLECT** | 200ms | Issuer nodes scan for new pending orders on-chain |
| **PROPOSE** | 200ms | Leader proposes a batch of collected orders |
| **SIGN_SUBMIT** | 200ms | Peer issuer nodes validate and BLS-sign the batch; leader aggregates and submits |
| **CONFIRM** | 200ms | Batch confirmation transaction is processed on-chain |
| **REBALANCE** | 200ms | Any pending rebalance operations are executed |

<Info>
The 1-second cycle means an order is typically batched within 1-2 seconds of submission. End-to-end settlement (including exchange execution) usually completes in under 10 seconds.
</Info>

## Order States

Each order transitions through these states:

```text
PENDING  -->  BATCHED  -->  FILLED
   |
   +-------->  TIMEOUT  (after 60s, up to 3 retries)
```

| State | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `PENDING` | Order submitted and USDC/tokens escrowed. Waiting for next cycle. |
| `BATCHED` | Included in a confirmed batch with valid BLS signature. AP is executing. |
| `FILLED` | AP reported fills, tokens minted/burned, settlement complete. |
| `TIMEOUT` | Order was not filled within 60 seconds. Retried up to 3 times before escrowed funds are returned. |

## Safeguards

### BLS Consensus

Every batch must be approved by a supermajority of the issuer nodes. The threshold is `ceil(2n/3)` -- for example, with 3 issuer nodes, at least 2 must sign. With 20 issuer nodes, at least 14 must sign.

<Warning>
BLS verification is **never bypassed** -- not in production, not in testing, not in local development. This is a core security invariant of the protocol.
</Warning>

### Price Tolerance

The AP must execute trades within **0.1%** of the reference price. The `confirmFills()` function on-chain validates every fill against the tolerance. If the AP reports a price outside the band, the fill is rejected.

```text
|reference_price - fill_price| / reference_price <= 0.001
```

### Order Timeout

Orders that are not filled within **60 seconds** enter a timeout flow:

1. First timeout: order is automatically retried (re-enters PENDING).
2. Second timeout: retried again.
3. Third timeout: order is cancelled. Escrowed USDC or ITP tokens are returned to the user.

<Note>
Timeouts are rare under normal conditions. They primarily protect against exchange downtime or network congestion.
</Note>

### Issuer-AP Separation

Issuer nodes and the Authorized Participant (AP) operate independently. Issuer nodes **cannot** communicate directly with the AP -- issuer nodes only read on-chain fill data. The issuer-AP separation prevents collusion between the consensus layer and the execution layer.

## Netting

When the issuer nodes collect orders in a cycle, the netting engine applies **netting** before sending trade requests to the AP. If there are $10,000 in buys and $7,000 in sells for the same ITP, only the net $3,000 buy needs to be executed on the exchange. Netting reduces trading costs and exchange exposure.
