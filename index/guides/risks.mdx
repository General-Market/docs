# Risks

Index Tracking Products (ITPs) track baskets of crypto assets. They are not risk-free. This page covers every material risk you should understand before committing capital.

---

## Market Risks

### NAV Fluctuation

An ITP's Net Asset Value (NAV) floats with the prices of its underlying assets. If the basket drops 50%, your ITP is worth 50% less. There is no floor or guaranteed value.

```
NAV = sum(qty[i] * price[i]) / 1e18
```

The NAV formula is transparent and deterministic — but it moves in real time with crypto markets.

### Concentration Risk

An ITP with few assets (e.g., 3 tokens) has high concentration risk. If one token collapses, the impact is significant. Market-cap-weighted ITPs naturally concentrate into the largest assets, which can be better or worse depending on market conditions.

<Tip>
Use equal-weight or capped-market-cap strategies to limit single-asset exposure. The backtesting engine lets you compare concentration vs. diversification before deploying.
</Tip>

### Drawdown

Crypto markets routinely experience -50% to -80% drawdowns. An ITP holding altcoins can draw down even more. Check the `max_drawdown_pct` from backtesting before deploying — it tells you the worst historical pain.

### Delistings

If an asset in the basket gets delisted from exchanges, it becomes illiquid. The ITP still holds it, but the price feed may freeze or drop to zero. This affects NAV directly.

---

## Structural Risks

### No Auto-Rebalance

After deploying an ITP, it does **not** auto-rebalance. The quantities are fixed until someone triggers a rebalance through the protocol. If the market shifts significantly and nobody rebalances, the ITP drifts from its target weights.

### Rebalance Costs

Each rebalance incurs trading fees (spread + gas). Frequent rebalancing in volatile markets can erode returns. The backtesting engine's `total_fees_pct` metric shows cumulative fee impact.

### Price Feed Risk

ITP NAV depends on price feeds from CoinGecko, Binance, and other sources aggregated by the data-node. If a price feed is stale or manipulated, the NAV computation will be incorrect. The issuer nodes have a 0.1% price tolerance to catch deviations.

---

## Smart Contract Risks

### BLS Consensus Dependency

All ITP operations (buy, sell, rebalance) require BLS multi-signature consensus from the issuer set. If fewer than 2/3 of issuers are online, operations stall. The protocol doesn't halt — it just can't process new orders until quorum is restored.

### Contract Bugs

The smart contracts (Index.sol, ITP.sol, BridgeProxy) are deployed on Arbitrum. Any bugs could result in loss of funds. Review the [contract architecture](/index/architecture/contracts) for details on the security model.

### Bridge Risk

If using bridged assets from L1 or other chains, the bridge introduces additional trust assumptions. Bridge failures or exploits can result in depegged collateral.

## Lending Risks (Morpho)

### Liquidation

If you borrow USDC against ITP collateral via Morpho, you're subject to liquidation if the ITP's value drops below the required health factor. Crypto can move fast — monitor positions actively.

### Health Factor

```
health_factor = (collateral_value * LTV) / debt
```

When `health_factor < 1.0`, the position is liquidatable. There is no grace period.

### Oracle Risk

Morpho relies on oracle prices. If oracle prices diverge from market reality, liquidations can be triggered incorrectly (or fail to trigger when they should).

## Checklist: Before Buying an ITP

<Steps>
  <Step title="Read the Holdings">
    Check what assets are in the basket and their weights. A "DeFi Index" could hold anything — look at the actual composition via the portfolio view or API.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check the Backtest">
    Run or review a backtest with the same category and weighting strategy. Pay attention to max drawdown and Sharpe ratio, not just total return.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Understand the Rebalance Schedule">
    Who triggers rebalances? How often? An ITP with no rebalance activity drifts from its target allocation over time.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Size Your Position">
    Don't put more into an ITP than you can afford to lose entirely. Start with a small position and scale up after observing the product's behavior across market conditions.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Monitor After Buying">
    Check the NAV periodically. The portfolio page shows current value, PnL, and asset breakdown. Set your own mental stop-loss.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Checklist: Before Creating an ITP

<Steps>
  <Step title="Backtest the Strategy">
    Use the [backtesting guide](/index/guides/backtesting) to simulate performance. Run a sweep across weighting strategies and top_n values.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check Asset Availability">
    All assets must have active price feeds. Use `/sim/categories` to see which assets are in the tradeable universe.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Realistic Fees">
    When backtesting, use `spread_multiplier: 2.0` for illiquid altcoins. The default 0.1% underestimates real-world slippage on thin order books.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Plan Your Rebalance Cadence">
    Decide upfront how often you'll trigger rebalances. Monthly is a common baseline. Threshold-based (drift > 5%) is more capital-efficient but requires active monitoring.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Communicate to Buyers">
    Add a clear name and description. Buyers need to understand the thesis (e.g., "Top 10 L1s by market cap, monthly rebalance") without diving into the contract.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Risk Summary

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| Market downturn | **High** | Diversify, check max drawdown in backtests |
| Concentration risk | Medium | Use equal-weight or capped strategies |
| Delistings | Medium | Prefer large-cap assets, monitor holdings |
| No auto-rebalance | Medium | Set reminders, check weight drift periodically |
| Price feed staleness | Low | Issuer 0.1% tolerance catches most issues |
| Contract bug | Low | Limited to deposited amount |
| Lending liquidation | **High** | Monitor health factor, keep buffer above 1.5x |
| Bridge risk | Medium | Prefer native assets where possible |
